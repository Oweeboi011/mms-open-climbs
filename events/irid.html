Location: Brgy. Sta. Ines, Gen. Nakar, Quezon (Rizal–Quezon border)
Features: 11 river crossings (one way), dense mossy forests, limestone rock formations, Kinabuan Falls

Mt. Irid is the highest peak in Rizal at 1,467 MASL. This Major Climb (Difficulty 6/9) is known for its challenging 11 river crossings to reach the basecamp at Sitio Sadlak, a stunning mossy forest on the summit trail, and dramatic limestone formations near the peak.
🗺

Water Source Information

  • Station 1 — Agos River tributaries: Abundant but not for drinking due to community and livestock upstream. Use for cooling off or washing only.
  • Station 2 — Kinabuan Falls: Reliable source for cooking or washing; treat or boil before drinking.
  • Station 3 — Sitio Sadlak Basecamp: Spring or pipe water near the bunkhouse. Refill here for dinner and summit assault.
  • Summit Trail: No water sources from basecamp to summit. Carry at least 1.5 L for the assault.

Route Summary
1
Starting Point (Jumpoff)
Begin at 180m in Brgy. Sta. Ines after military clearance. Prepare trekking sandals for 11 river crossings ahead.
2
River Crossings & Kinabuan Falls
Navigate 11 river crossings through the Agos River tributaries. Stop at Kinabuan Falls for lunch and a swim before the ascent steepens.
3
Basecamp — Sitio Sadlak (800m)
Arrive at basecamp at ~800m. Set up camp, refill water, and rest for the early morning summit assault.
4
Summit Assault — Mossy Forest
Gain 667m through dense mossy forest and dramatic limestone rock formations. The steepest section of the climb.
5
Summit (1,467m)
Reach the highest peak of Rizal province with sweeping views of the Sierra Madre range. Rest and capture the moment before descending.

Weather & Forecast

Gen. Nakar and the Sierra Madre range receive heavy rainfall year-round. July is within the rainy season — river levels can rise significantly after rains. Check conditions and river status 24–48 hours before departure.

🌬 Windy.com 🌦 PAG-ASA Forecast ⛅ Weather.com

⚠ River crossings may become impassable during or after heavy rains. The climb lead reserves the right to cancel or postpone. Monitor PAG-ASA Tropical Cyclone bulletins closely.

Essential Gear & Reminders

Footwear Strategy
Use trekking sandals for Day 1 (11 river crossings). Switch to hiking shoes with good grip at the basecamp for the muddy and rocky summit assault on Day 2.
🐛 Limatik (Blood Leeches)
Mt. Irid is notorious for blood leeches, especially during the rainy season. Bring alcohol or salt to safely remove them. Do not pull them off forcefully.
👤 Military Clearance Required
A courtesy call and clearance from the local military detachment in Brgy. Sta. Ines is required before starting the hike. The climb lead must coordinate this in advance.
💧 Waterproofing is Essential
Place all electronics, clothes, and documents in dry bags. River crossings can reach waist-deep depending on recent rainfall. Assume everything will get wet.

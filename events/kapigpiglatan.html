Features: Ridge trails, river crossings, panoramic views, and Veto Falls

Known as the "Little Bukidnon of Zambales," Mt. Kapigpiglatan is celebrated for its rolling hills, golden cogon grasslands, and the scenic Veto Falls. A 2-day Summit-to-Surf itinerary allows hikers to enjoy the mountain and then relax at the nearby beaches of San Narciso or San Felipe.
🗺

Water Source Information

⚠ Caution — Water Potability
Natural water sources are not recommended for drinking even if they look clear. The area upstream is a grazing ground for carabaos and cattle. If you plan to use water from the falls for cooking or drinking, a high-quality filtration system or boiling is mandatory.
  • Rivers at Base: Available for cooling off or washing; not for drinking.
  • Veto Falls (Descent): Reliable water source — treat or boil before drinking.
  • Carry at least 2–3 L per person for the entire hike.

Weather & Forecast

San Narciso, Zambales has a tropical climate. July falls within the rainy season — expect afternoon showers and humid conditions. Check the forecast 24–48 hours before departure.

🌬 Windy.com 🌦 PAG-ASA Forecast ⛅ Weather.com

⚠ Monitor PAG-ASA Tropical Cyclone bulletins, especially during July–September. The climb may be cancelled or rescheduled due to bad weather.
